Potato Soup

Description

This simple and delicious potato soup will have your family going back for seconds and thirds!

This dish is easy to prep and so simple to cook. Put this on the rotation for a 30-45 minute weeknight meal with leftovers!

Ingredients

  • Bacon (6 strips) cut into small pieces
  • 3 Tablespoons butter (salted or unsalted)
  • 1 Medium yellow onion chopped
  • 3 Large garlic cloves minced (2 tbs of a pre-minced jar)
  • 1/3 Cup flour
  • 2 1/2 lbs gold potatoes peeled and diced into to 1" pieces
  • 4 Cups chicken broth
  • 2 Cups milk
  • 2/3 Cup heavy cream
  • 1 1/2 Teaspoon salt (or to taste)
  • 1 Teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1/4-1/2 Teaspoon ancho chili powder
  • 2/3 Cup sour cream

Steps

  1. Slice bacon into 1-2" pieces, place into large Dutch Oven or soup pot over medium heat and cook until bacon is crisp and browned.
  2. Remove bacon and set aside, leaving the fat in the pot.
  3. Add butter and chopped onion to fat and cook over medium heat until onions are translucent and tender (3-5 minutes)
  4. Add garlic and cook until fragrant (about 30 seconds)
  5. Sprinkle the flour over the ingredients in the pot and stir until smooth(use whisk if needed)
  6. Add diced potatoes to the pot along with chicken broth, milk heavy cream, ancho chili powder, and salt and pepper to taste. Stir well.
  7. Bring to a boil and cook until potatoes are tender when pierced with a fork (about 10 minutes)
  8. Reduce heat to simmer and blend about half the soup (about 5 cups) until smooth. You can transfer this amount to a blender (Caution: Hot Soup!) Or you can use an immersion blender.
  9. Mix bacon pieces into soup and reserve some for a topping if you like.
  10. Top with your choice of cheese, sour cream, bacon, scallions, anything you want!
